CGPA Calculation Formula

CGPA = Σ(Credit × Grade Point) / Σ(Credit)

By Subjects Method:

  • Σ(Credit × Grade Point) = Sum of (credit × grade point) for all subjects across all semesters
  • Σ(Credit) = Total credits across all subjects and semesters
  • Combines all semesters' subjects into one calculation for true cumulative average

By SGPA Method:

  • CGPA = Σ(SGPA × Semester Credits) / Σ(Semester Credits)
  • Calculates weighted average of SGPAs across semesters
  • Each semester's SGPA is weighted by its total credits

Example Calculation: If Semester 1 SGPA=8.5 (20 credits) and Semester 2 SGPA=9.0 (20 credits):

CGPA = (8.5×20 + 9.0×20) / (20+20) = (170 + 180) / 40 = 350/40 = 8.75

Two Calculation Methods Explained

Method 1: By Subjects

Best for: Accurate CGPA calculation combining all subjects

How it works:

  1. Enter all subjects for each semester
  2. Add each subject's credits and grade
  3. Calculator combines all semesters' data
  4. Computes true cumulative GPA

Calculation: Σ(All Credits × Grades) / Σ(All Credits)

Method 2: By SGPA

Best for: Quick calculation using existing SGPAs

How it works:

  1. You already know each semester's SGPA
  2. Enter SGPA and credits per semester
  3. Calculator weights SGPAs by credits
  4. Computes cumulative average

Calculation: Σ(SGPA × Semester Credits) / Σ(Credits)

Importance of CGPA in Your Academic Career

Campus Placements

Most Fortune 500 companies filter candidates based on minimum CGPA (typically 7.0+). Your CGPA determines eligibility for interviews.

Higher Education

Universities worldwide consider CGPA for MS/PhD admissions. Scholarships and educational loans require strong CGPA.

Scholarship & Awards

Merit scholarships, academic honors, and recognition certificates are based on CGPA. High CGPA unlocks financial aid opportunities.

Professional Development

Your CGPA is a reflection of your dedication. High CGPA demonstrates commitment and discipline valued by employers.

Understanding SGPA vs CGPA

Aspect SGPA (Semester GPA) CGPA (Cumulative GPA)
Time Period Single semester only All semesters combined
Calculation Credits and grades of one semester All semester data or SGPAs combined
Subjects Included Semester subjects only All subjects across all semesters
Impact Reflects semester performance Overall academic performance
Use Case Semester evaluation, internal assessments Placements, admissions, scholarships

Key Point: Good CGPA requires consistent performance across all semesters. Your earlier SGPAs are equally weighted in CGPA calculation, so improving your score now will gradually improve your overall CGPA.
